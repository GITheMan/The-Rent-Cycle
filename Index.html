PayDay Respect Tradition

A tradition carried by generations of honorable military men—and those who carry their legacy

What It's About

This tradition comes from generations of military Paw Paws who offered PayDay candy bars to women and girls—not as flirtation, but as a gesture of respect, honor, and recognition.

Why I Carry It

I'm not in the military—but I am family to military. It doesn’t matter. This is something I am obliged to carry on as a good citizen, in honor of my colors and stripes.

The Meaning

Offering a PayDay bar is a small but powerful way to say: "Even if the world doesn’t pay you right, here’s a little honor from mine."
